# Spirit Airlines Collapses as Iran Tensions Shake Markets

## Lead

Spirit Airlines has shut down after a last-minute rescue deal failed, marking the first major airline bankruptcy in the United States since the pandemic-era consolidation wave. The closure strands thousands of passengers and signals ongoing distress in the budget airline sector despite record travel demand.

For investors, the airline's failure adds to broader concerns about consumer spending resilience and corporate debt loads. Simultaneously, escalating U.S.-Iran military tensions, including a naval blockade and fresh strike threats from President Trump, are pushing oil prices higher and shaking confidence in risk assets heading into the new week.

## Market Snapshot

- S&P 500: Fell 1.2 percent as geopolitical risk offset strong Berkshire earnings.
- FTSE 100: Edged down 0.4 percent amid global uncertainty.
- Nasdaq: Dropped 1.5 percent with tech and airline stocks leading declines.

## In Brief

- SPIRIT AIRLINES: The budget carrier ceased operations immediately after a rescue financing deal collapsed, leaving passengers stranded and highlighting the aviation sector's ongoing structural challenges.

- US-IRAN CONFLICT: President Trump confirmed the U.S. could resume military strikes on Iran while the Navy imposed a blockade on Iranian ports, sending crude prices surging as diplomatic channels remain open.

- FEDERAL RESERVE: Chair Powell faced unprecedented dissent at the FOMC meeting as committee members signaled fewer rate cuts ahead, rattling bond markets and dampening Wall Street's hopes for easing.

- BERKSHIRE HATHAWAY: Warren Buffett's conglomerate reported operating earnings more than doubling to a record level, though the company also built its cash pile to an all-time $397 billion.

## Watch Tomorrow

Traders will monitor Iranian port activity and any escalation in U.S. military positioning as oil markets brace for potential supply disruptions.
